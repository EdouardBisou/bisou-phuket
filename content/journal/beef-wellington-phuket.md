## What is Beef Wellington, exactly?

Beef Wellington is one of the great showpieces of European cooking. At its heart sits a center cut of beef fillet, the most tender part of the animal. The fillet is seared, then layered with a duxelles, a finely chopped mixture of mushrooms, shallots and herbs cooked down slowly until it turns dark and almost jammy. A thin veil of cured ham, often prosciutto, wraps the beef and the duxelles together. The whole parcel is then enclosed in pastry and baked until golden.

The result, when it is done properly, is a study in contrast. A crisp, burnished crust gives way to the savory mushroom layer, and then to beef that should still be rosy and yielding at the center. It is rich, but it is also precise. Every layer has a job.

The dish carries a French accent for good reason. The discipline behind it, the searing, the duxelles, the careful timing of the pastry, is classical French technique through and through. That technique is exactly the language we speak at Bisou.
